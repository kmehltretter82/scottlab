ScottLab — an interactive Scott information systems playground

ScottLab teaches Scott information systems — the constructive presentation of domain theory from Dana S. Scott's “Domains for Denotational Semantics” (1982) — through short, hands-on browser lessons. It answers questions such as: what does a program mean before it finishes? How can a computation work with an answer it only partly knows? Why does take 3 work on an infinite list?

The guided lessons

  1. Bottom. Meet ⊥, the state with no specific information yet — and learn why it is not an error, a contradiction, or Boolean false.
  2. Tokens. Look inside ⊥ and meet finite observations: a token is one piece of information, a state is a consistent collection of them.
  3. Consistency. Try to combine incompatible observations and inspect the concrete conflict witness.
  4. The information order. Move through the states of a flat Boolean, ordered by how much they know.
  5. Formalisation. Meet the explicit distinguished token Δ and the tuple A = (T, Δ, Con, ⊢), and derive closure(∅) = {Δ} = ⊥.
  6. Entailment. Watch rule-driven entailment closure run step by step with an inspectable derivation trace.
  7. States. Learn what separates an arbitrary selection of tokens from a consistent, entailment-closed state.
  8. Continuous maps. Apply a finite-generator approximable mapping and see why it is monotone and Scott continuous.

A read-only sandbox lets you explore the same systems freely, with a synchronized state diagram, token tray, definition, and closure explanation. Everything works with mouse, touch, or keyboard, respects reduced motion, and is available in English and German.

Beyond the lessons: a least-fixed-point lesson answers “why does take 3 work on an infinite list?”, a capstone draws the eleven-state function space [Bool → Bool], a game lesson solves a take-away game by retrograde analysis, and an example gallery imports, exports, and quizzes you on your own systems.

Static companion pages: a concise mathematical reference and a guide for instructors with embeddable widgets.

ScottLab is free and open source under the Apache-2.0 license: source repository. This page is an interactive application — enable JavaScript to start the lessons.
